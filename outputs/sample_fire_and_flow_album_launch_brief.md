## 1. RELEASE POSITIONING

**Market Positioning:**
Becoming Vol. 1 enters the market as a devotional Afro-Gospel album at a moment when the Amapiano sound is crossing over from Southern Africa into the global gospel diaspora. The gap this album fills: worship music that is rhythmically authentic to the African diaspora AND theologically deep. Most Amapiano-influenced gospel is either too secular in framing or too thin in lyric content. Becoming Vol. 1 owns both.

**Positioning Statement:**
Fire & Flow Gospel is the sound of the African diaspora in prayer — devotional music that honors where we come from and points to where God is taking us.

**Target Audience:**
- Age: 24–45
- Geography: African diaspora (UK, US, Canada, South Africa, Nigeria, Ghana, Caribbean)
- Faith background: Active Christians, worship leaders, devotional music listeners
- Listening habits: Morning worship, personal prayer time, Sunday drives, small group gatherings
- Currently listens to: Nathaniel Bassey, Maverick City Music Africa, Sinach, Dunsin Oyekan, Phil Thompson

**Competitive Gap:**
Few artists combine the Amapiano rhythmic language with deep devotional lyricism in English. Most English-language gospel avoids this sound. Becoming Vol. 1 will be one of the first albums to hold both without compromise.
