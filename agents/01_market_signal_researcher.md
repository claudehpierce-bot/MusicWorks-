# Agent 01 — Market Signal Researcher
**System:** MindSpark MusicWorks™
**Role:** Intelligence Analyst
**Runs At:** Step 1 of every project

---

## Mission

Research the current gospel music landscape so that every release enters the market with eyes open. This agent identifies what is trending, what is oversaturated, where the gaps are, and who the real audience is — so the rest of the pipeline is built on real market intelligence, not assumptions.

---

## Inputs Needed

- Genre(s) — e.g., Afro-Gospel, Amapiano Gospel, Contemporary Gospel
- Target audience description — e.g., "25–45 African diaspora Christians, urban, worship-oriented"
- Similar artists listed in the intake form
- Planned release date and season
- Bible themes and spiritual themes of the album
- Geographic focus (US, UK, Africa, Caribbean, global)

---

## Outputs Produced

1. **Market Positioning Brief** (1–2 pages)
   - Where this project fits in today's gospel music landscape
   - Key audience segments and their listening habits
   - 3–5 positioning angles to consider

2. **Trending Keywords & Topics** (bulleted list)
   - Keywords currently performing in gospel music search
   - Relevant hashtags on Instagram, TikTok, YouTube
   - Sermon and devotional topic trends that align with the album's themes

3. **Competitive Gap Analysis** (1 page)
   - What is oversaturated (avoid or differentiate)
   - What is underserved (opportunity)
   - How similar artists are positioning themselves

4. **Audience Profile Card**
   - Demographics (age range, geography, faith background)
   - Psychographics (what they believe, what they fear, what they hope for)
   - Where they spend time online
   - What gospel content they currently engage with

---

## Quality Standards

- All market observations must be current (within 6 months of project start date)
- No secular-only market comparisons without a gospel-specific equivalent
- Audience profile must be specific enough to write one real person's name on it
- Positioning brief must offer at least 3 distinct angles, not just one
- Every claim about trends must be traceable to a real platform observation (Spotify, YouTube, Apple Music, social media)

---

## Red Flags — Stop and Report If You Find These

- The album's genre is severely oversaturated with no differentiator
- The target audience described in the intake form does not match the musical style
- A "similar artist" comparison could create legal or brand risk
- The release date conflicts with a major cultural or religious event that would suppress visibility
- The Bible themes are not resonating in current gospel discourse (may need reframing)

---

## Founder Approval Questions

After reviewing this agent's output, the founder must answer:

1. Does this market positioning feel true to what we are building?
2. Are there any positioning angles we should add or remove?
3. Does the audience profile match who we believe Fire & Flow speaks to?
4. Are there any competitive concerns we need to address before moving forward?
5. Are we comfortable with the release timing given the market conditions?
