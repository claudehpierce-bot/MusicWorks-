## What Is the Campaign Engine?

The Campaign Engine governs the intensity, frequency, and focus of every marketing activity. It answers the question: "How hard are we pushing, and in which direction?" The founder selects a campaign mode before Phase 1 begins. The mode determines posting frequency, platform priority, team effort required, and what success looks like.

Different releases call for different modes. A devotional EP requires a different energy than a chart-push single. A ministry project requires different KPIs than a streaming growth campaign. The Campaign Engine gives the founder five clearly defined operating modes to choose from — and permission to use only one at a time.

**Current mode for Becoming Vol. 1:** Blitz (launch week) → transitioning to Ministry Push (Months 2–3)

---

## CAMPAIGN MODE 1: STANDARD

### Purpose
Sustainable, consistent audience building. The default mode for a new independent artist who wants to establish presence without burning out. Designed for long-term momentum rather than a sharp launch spike.

### When to Use
- A new artist releasing their first project with a small or nonexistent audience
- An artist who cannot sustain high posting frequency
- A release that is designed to build a catalog rather than chase a single moment
- A re-release or anniversary edition

### Posting Frequency
| Platform | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| Instagram | 3x per week |
| Facebook | 2x per week |
| YouTube Shorts / Reels | 2x per week |
| YouTube (long-form) | 1x per week or every 2 weeks |
| TikTok | 3x per week |
| Email | 1x per week during launch month; 2x per month thereafter |

### Primary Platforms
Instagram, YouTube, Email

### Secondary Platforms
Facebook, TikTok

### KPIs
| Metric | Standard Mode Target (30 days) |
|--------|-------------------------------|
| Total Streams (Spotify) | 500–2,000 |
| Spotify Saves | 50+ |
| YouTube Views | 500–1,500 |
| Email List Growth | +15–30 subscribers |
| Devotional Downloads | 15–40 |

### Success Indicators
- Consistent week-over-week stream growth (even small growth counts)
- At least 2 organic shares or unsolicited testimonials
- Email open rate above 25%
- At least 1 playlist curator response

### Founder Approval Points
- Week 1: Review engagement rate and decide whether to stay in Standard or upgrade to Growth
- Month 1: Review all KPIs against Standard targets; decide whether the campaign needs a boost

### Resource Requirement
- Low-Medium: 5–8 hours per week of content execution time
- Can be managed by one person

## CAMPAIGN MODE 2: GROWTH

### Purpose
Deliberate audience expansion. The artist has a small existing audience and wants to grow it meaningfully with this release. Growth mode combines consistent organic content with modest paid amplification of top-performing posts.

### When to Use
- An artist with an existing audience of 500+ followers who wants to break through to the next level
- A second or third release building on an established catalog
- When the goal is new listener acquisition, not just engagement with existing fans

### Posting Frequency
| Platform | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| Instagram | 5x per week |
| Facebook | 3x per week |
| YouTube Shorts / Reels | 4x per week |
| YouTube (long-form) | 1x per week |
| TikTok | 5x per week |
| Email | 2x per week during launch; weekly thereafter |

### Primary Platforms
Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts (short-form drives discovery)

### Secondary Platforms
Email (retention), YouTube long-form (depth), Facebook (church/ministry demographic)

### Paid Amplification Component
- Boost top-performing organic posts with $5–$20/day budget for 3–5 days
- Target: people who follow similar gospel artists; African diaspora demographic; 25–45 age range
- Never boost a post that hasn't already proven organic engagement — amplify what is working, not what you hope will work

### KPIs
| Metric | Growth Mode Target (30 days) |
|--------|------------------------------|
| Total Streams (Spotify) | 2,000–8,000 |
| New Spotify Monthly Listeners | 200+ |
| Spotify Saves | 150+ |
| YouTube Views | 2,000–6,000 |
| Email List Growth | +50–100 subscribers |
| Social Following Growth | +100–300 followers (combined) |
| Devotional Downloads | 50–100 |

### Success Indicators
- New listeners from geographies not in the existing audience
- At least 1 independent playlist placement
- Email open rate sustained above 28%
- Top-performing boosted post generates 5x+ organic reach vs. unboosted posts

### Founder Approval Points
- Before any paid spend: founder approves the ad creative and targeting parameters
- Week 2: Review paid ad performance vs. organic — adjust or pause if ROI is poor
- Month 1: Full KPI review against Growth targets

### Resource Requirement
- Medium-High: 8–12 hours per week execution
- Budget required: $50–$200/month minimum for paid amplification
- Ideally managed by 2 people (one for content, one for analytics)

## CAMPAIGN MODE 3: BLITZ

### Purpose
Maximum concentrated output during a defined, short window — typically launch week. Blitz mode is not sustainable long-term. It is designed to create a single moment of significant noise and generate enough early streaming data to activate algorithmic discovery tools on platforms like Spotify and YouTube.

### When to Use
- Launch week of any major release (album, EP, or flagship single)
- A viral moment opportunity that needs to be capitalized on quickly
- A strategic push tied to a major event (conference, church anniversary, gospel awards season)

**Note:** Blitz mode is never the entire campaign. It is always followed by a transition to Standard or Ministry Push mode within 7–10 days.

### Posting Frequency
| Platform | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| Instagram | Daily (1–2 posts + Stories) |
| Facebook | Daily |
| YouTube Shorts / Reels | Daily |
| YouTube (long-form) | 2–3x during the week |
| TikTok | 2x per day if possible |
| Email | 2 emails during launch week (launch day + day 3) |

### Primary Platforms
All platforms simultaneously — the goal is omnipresence during the window

### Secondary
None — Blitz does not have secondary platforms; everything is active

### KPIs (7-Day Blitz Window)
| Metric | Blitz Mode Target (7 days) |
|--------|---------------------------|
| Total Streams | 500–2,000 |
| Spotify Saves (Day 1–7) | 100+ |
| YouTube Views (Week 1) | 500–2,000 |
| Email Open Rate (Launch Email) | 30%+ |
| Playlist Curator Responses | 3+ responses (yes or no) |
| Social Impressions | 5,000+ (combined) |
| Devotional Downloads | 25+ |

### Success Indicators
- Spotify Release Radar delivery confirmed (requires 7-day advance pitch submission)
- YouTube video above 500 views in first 72 hours
- At least one organic share from a gospel influencer or ministry account
- Spotify daily streams maintain above 50/day for the first 7 days

### Founder Approval Points
- Pre-Blitz: Founder reviews and approves every piece of content for launch week before the week begins
- Day 3: Founder checks stream data — if numbers are significantly above benchmark, consider activating Chart Push mode
- Day 7: Founder reviews Blitz performance and selects the next campaign mode

### Resource Requirement
- High: 15–20 hours in launch week for one person
- This is a full-effort week. No other major projects should be active.

### Blitz Transition Rules
After 7 days in Blitz mode, the campaign transitions automatically to either:
- **Standard** (if resource-constrained or if the project is a long-catalog builder)
- **Ministry Push** (if the primary goal is church/ministry penetration)
- **Growth** (if early data shows strong new listener acquisition potential)

## CAMPAIGN MODE 4: CHART PUSH

### Purpose
Coordinate all promotional activity to maximize streaming numbers within a specific window, with the goal of hitting algorithmic thresholds that trigger Spotify's Discover Weekly, Release Radar, and Editorial consideration. Chart Push mode is commercially focused.

### When to Use
- When early launch data (Day 3–7) shows significantly higher-than-expected stream velocity
- When there is a real possibility of entering a gospel chart or playlist based on current trajectory
- When an artist wants to use this release as a proof point for a label deal, sync placement, or radio consideration

**Important Caveat:** Chart Push mode is not appropriate for every release or every artist. For Fire & Flow Gospel, the ministry mission should not be sacrificed for chart numbers. Only activate Chart Push mode if streaming velocity genuinely warrants it — not as a vanity exercise.

### Posting Frequency
Same as Blitz, but with additional coordination:
- Coordinated streaming push (ask email list, community, and personal network to stream at a specific time on a specific day)
- Coordinated save push (saves influence algorithmic delivery more than streams alone)

### Critical Actions Unique to Chart Push
- [ ] Send a "stream together" email to the full list with a specific day and time
- [ ] Post a coordinated call-to-action: "Stream [Track Title] right now — link in bio"
- [ ] Ask 10–20 personal contacts to save the album on Spotify specifically
- [ ] Engage all church/ministry contacts to play the album in a group setting (group streams count)

### KPIs
| Metric | Chart Push Target |
|--------|------------------|
| Spotify streams (7-day) | 5,000+ |
| Spotify saves (7-day) | 500+ |
| Spotify monthly listeners | 1,000+ |
| Discover Weekly inclusion | Goal — not guaranteed |
| Gospel chart appearance | Goal — not guaranteed |

### Success Indicators
- Spotify for Artists shows "Added to playlist" notifications
- Discover Weekly or Release Radar impressions visible in analytics
- Streams-per-day maintaining or increasing rather than declining after Day 7

### Founder Approval Points
- Before activating: Founder must consciously choose this mode and be comfortable with the commercial-first focus
- Mid-push: Is the message of the music being communicated, or is it becoming only numbers? Course-correct if needed.

### Resource Requirement
- Very High: Requires full-team coordination, significant personal outreach, and daily monitoring
- Budget: Paid promotion strongly recommended in this mode ($200–$500/month)

## CAMPAIGN MODE 5: MINISTRY PUSH

### Purpose
Activate the church, ministry, and devotional community as the primary distribution channel. This mode prioritizes devotional impact, church adoption, and small group engagement over streaming numbers. It is the most unique mode in the MusicWorks system — no secular music platform offers an equivalent.

### When to Use
- Month 2–3 of any campaign, after the initial launch energy subsides
- A project that is primarily designed as a ministry resource (devotional album, worship resource, prayer companion)
- When reaching church music directors, pastors, and ministry leaders is the primary goal
- Always alongside the final phase of any Fire & Flow campaign

### Posting Frequency
| Platform | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| Instagram | 3x per week — scripture and devotional focus |
| Facebook | 3x per week — church demographic primary |
| YouTube | 1x per week — devotional or "behind the song" format |
| Email | Weekly — ministry-focused content |
| TikTok | 2x per week — optional, scripture-forward content |

### Primary Platforms
Facebook (highest engagement with 35+ church demographic), Email, YouTube

### Secondary Platforms
Instagram (younger worship community), direct outreach (personal messages to ministry contacts)

### Unique Actions in Ministry Push Mode
- [ ] Send personal outreach to 5–10 pastors or worship leaders per week
- [ ] Offer the devotional guide as a free church resource (not as a streaming promotion)
- [ ] Submit the album for CCLI registration and communicate this to churches
- [ ] Post "How to use this album in your small group" content
- [ ] Share short testimonials from believers about how specific tracks affected their prayer life
- [ ] Offer chord charts or lead sheets to worship teams (if available)

### KPIs
| Metric | Ministry Push Target (30 days) |
|--------|-------------------------------|
| Devotional Downloads | 100+ |
| Church/Ministry Inquiries | 5+ |
| CCLI Registration | Complete and communicated |
| Devotional Shares | 10+ (social shares of the devotional guide) |
| Facebook Engagement Rate | 5%+ on ministry-focused posts |
| Email Open Rate | 30%+ (ministry content typically outperforms promotional) |

### Ministry-Specific Success Indicators
- A pastor tells you the album was used in a Sunday service
- A small group leader says they used the devotional guide with their group
- A school or youth ministry uses the music in a devotional context
- A believer sends a personal message about how the music affected their prayer life or spiritual growth

**Note:** These indicators cannot be measured in a Spotify dashboard. They are measured in conversations, messages, and personal testimony. They are the most important metrics MusicWorks tracks.

### Founder Approval Points
- Before sending any church outreach: founder reviews and approves all messaging
- Monthly: founder reviews ministry inquiry log and decides which follow-ups to pursue
- End of campaign: founder writes a narrative summary of ministry impact alongside the data summary in Lessons Learned

### Resource Requirement
- Medium: 6–8 hours per week, but requires relationship investment that cannot be rushed
- No significant budget required — Ministry Push is relationship-driven, not paid-media-driven

## CAMPAIGN MODE SELECTION GUIDE

| Situation | Recommended Mode | Notes |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| First week of any release | Blitz | Always — then transition |
| Building a new audience from zero | Standard → Growth | Start Standard, upgrade if data warrants |
| Second release with existing audience | Growth | Build on what Becoming Vol. 1 established |
| Early data shows viral momentum | Chart Push | Only if data genuinely supports it |
| Month 2–3 of any campaign | Ministry Push | Default for Fire & Flow's long-term identity |
| Church/ministry-specific project | Ministry Push | From launch week |
| Limited time or team resources | Standard | Sustainable > unsustainable |

**For Becoming Vol. 1:**
- Launch week (July 3–10): **Blitz**
- Week 2–4 (July 10–31): **Standard** with Ministry Push elements
- Month 2–3 (August–September): **Ministry Push**
