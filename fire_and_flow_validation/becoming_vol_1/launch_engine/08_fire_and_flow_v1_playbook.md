## PART 1: WHO WE ARE (BRAND RULES)

Every campaign decision is governed by these brand rules. They cannot be overridden by a marketing trend, a platform algorithm, or a commercial pressure.

### The Non-Negotiables
1. **We never hide the AI-assisted nature of this work.** Transparency is our brand, not our liability.
2. **The Gospel is the product. The music is the vehicle.** If a campaign decision would compromise the message to improve the metrics, the campaign decision is wrong.
3. **Scripture is accurate or it is not used.** Verify every reference. Every. Time.
4. **Human approval precedes every public release.** The system suggests. The founder decides. Always.
5. **We build relationships, not just audiences.** Respond to real messages. Show up as a real person. Do not let the platform become the face.

### The Brand Voice in One Sentence
Warm, confident, spiritually grounded, credible — never preachy, never hype-driven, never apologetic about faith.

### What Fire & Flow Gospel Is NOT
- Not a hype machine
- Not a prosperity gospel voice
- Not an AI music generator
- Not a mainstream crossover act that happens to mention God
- Not a content factory

## PART 2: THE RELEASE SEQUENCE (EVERY RELEASE)

This sequence applies to every album, EP, or single released through Fire & Flow Gospel and the MusicWorks platform.

### Step 1: Green Light
The founder informally approves the project concept. ("Yes, we're making this.")

### Step 2: Intake
Complete the Album Intake Template in `/inputs/`. Every field. No rushing.

### Step 3: Pipeline (MusicWorks Core — Agents 01–13)
Run agents in sequence. Honor every hard gate. Do not proceed past Agent 03 (Theology) or Agent 04 (Audio QC) without signed certificates.

**Minimum pipeline timeline:**
- Full album: 6–10 weeks
- EP (4–6 tracks): 4–6 weeks
- Single: 2–3 weeks

### Step 4: Distribution Submission
Submit to distributor with minimum 4-week lead time. Ideal: 6–8 weeks.
Submit Spotify editorial pitch at the same time as distribution submission (minimum 7 days before release; 4 weeks recommended).

### Step 5: Pre-Launch Phase (3–4 Weeks Before Launch)
- Activate pre-save link
- Begin email warmup sequence
- Begin social teaser content (1–2 posts per week)
- Prepare all launch week content
- Send playlist outreach pitches
- Distribute press kit

### Step 6: Launch Week (Blitz Mode)
Follow the Weekly Operations Calendar (Document 05) for launch week day-by-day execution.

### Step 7: Sustain Phase (Day 8 → Day 90)
Transition to Standard or Ministry Push mode. Continue content, devotional outreach, and ministry engagement. Do not go silent.

### Step 8: 90-Day Review
Complete the Lessons Learned document (Document 07). Update the KPI dashboard. Write the Feed-Forward Summary.

### Step 9: Playbook Update
Update this playbook with any new standing rules, changed benchmarks, or revised processes learned from the completed release. Increment the version number.

## PART 3: CAMPAIGN DECISION GUIDE

Use this guide to make the most common campaign decisions quickly and consistently.

### Which Campaign Mode Should We Start In?

| Situation | Start Mode |
|-----------|-----------|
| First release, no audience | Blitz launch week → Standard |
| Second release, small audience | Blitz launch week → Growth |
| Third+ release, established audience | Blitz launch week → Growth or Chart Push |
| Ministry-specific project (devotional album, church resource) | Blitz launch week → Ministry Push |
| All campaigns, Month 2–3 | Ministry Push |

### How Much Should We Post?

| Mode | Posts Per Week (combined platforms) |
|------|--------------------------------------|
| Standard | 8–12 |
| Growth | 12–18 |
| Blitz | 20–30 |
| Ministry Push | 6–10 |

*Note: Quality is always more important than quantity. One excellent post beats three mediocre ones.*

### How Do We Respond to a Bad Day (Low Streams)?

This will happen. Here is the protocol:

1. Do not post a desperate "please stream this" message. This reads as needy and devalues the music.
2. Check the data: Is this one bad day, or a trend over 3+ days?
3. If one bad day: note it and continue. Algorithm fluctuation is normal.
4. If a 3-day declining trend: review the last 3 days of content. Was there a gap in posting? Did a post underperform? Identify and address.
5. If a 7-day declining trend: escalate to Founder Review. Consider whether a fresh content angle, a playlist push, or a ministry outreach wave would help.
6. Never compare this release's Day-X numbers to any other artist's Day-X numbers publicly.

### How Do We Respond to an Unexpectedly Good Day?

1. Record it in Running Notes immediately. What happened today that may have caused this?
2. Do not assume it will repeat. Study it instead.
3. Consider whether conditions can be recreated: did a specific post drive traffic? Did a playlist add happen?
4. If streams more than double the 7-day average on any single day, investigate whether a playlist or influencer shared the music without notifying you.
5. A good day is a data point, not a permanent shift. Stay consistent.

## PART 4: CONTENT ARCHITECTURE

Every piece of content for Fire & Flow Gospel fits into one of five content categories. Rotate through all five across a campaign.

### The Five Content Pillars

**Pillar 1: Scripture & Devotional**
*"Here is the Word of God that lives in this music."*
- Direct scripture posts with context
- "This verse changed how I see this song" posts
- Devotional questions for the audience
- Prayer prompts
- Scripture deep dives tied to specific tracks

**Pillar 2: Behind the Music**
*"Here is where this music came from."*
- Story of how a specific track was created
- The spiritual moment that inspired a lyric
- The creative decision behind the album sequence
- Production notes (kept accessible, not technical)

**Pillar 3: Ministry & Church Community**
*"Here is how this music serves the Church."*
- How to use the album in a small group
- Devotional guide promotional content
- Choir/worship team use cases
- Pastor and ministry leader testimonials
- Sunday morning invitation posts

**Pillar 4: Listener Voice**
*"Here is what this music is doing in real people's lives."*
- Testimonials (with permission)
- Response to comments that contain meaningful stories
- "What does becoming mean to you?" engagement questions
- Listener-generated content (sharing, if it happens)

**Pillar 5: Artist Identity**
*"Here is who Fire & Flow Gospel is."*
- Transparent AI/human story
- African diaspora cultural identity content
- Mission statement content ("Why we make music this way")
- Gratitude posts that are personal, not performative

### Content Rotation Rule
No more than two consecutive posts from the same pillar. The goal is a feed that is varied and multi-dimensional.

## PART 5: THE FIVE PLATFORMS — RULES AND PRIORITIES

### Spotify
**Role:** Primary discovery and streaming platform.
**Priority:** Highest for revenue and algorithmic growth.
**Actions:** Optimize artist profile, featured release, editorial pitch.
**Content:** None created here — music is the content.
**Watch:** Saves and playlist adds are more important than stream count.

### YouTube
**Role:** Long-form home for lyric videos, behind-the-song videos, and ministry content.
**Priority:** High — YouTube videos have the longest shelf life of any content format.
**Content:** Lyric videos, behind the song, devotional videos, shorts.
**Watch:** Average watch time. If people are clicking off early, the hook is wrong.

### Instagram
**Role:** Visual storytelling and community building. Primary platform for the 25–40 creative/faith audience.
**Priority:** High for community engagement.
**Best Format:** Reels outperform static posts 3:1 on reach. Use them.
**Watch:** Saves. Saves are a proxy for "this mattered enough to revisit."

### Facebook
**Role:** Church and ministry community. The 35–60 faith-engaged demographic lives here.
**Priority:** Medium-High for Ministry Push mode. Lower for Standard mode.
**Best Format:** Long-form posts with scripture perform better than short captions here.
**Watch:** Shares. If church-goers are sharing, it's reaching the ministry community.

### Email
**Role:** Direct relationship. No algorithm. Highest-intent audience.
**Priority:** Always high — email subscribers are the most valuable audience because they opted in deliberately.
**Rules:** One CTA per email. Scripture in every email. Never spam. Send less than you think you should.
**Watch:** Open rate. If it falls below 20%, something is wrong with the list, the subject lines, or the send frequency.

## PART 6: MINISTRY PARTNERSHIP PROTOCOL

Fire & Flow Gospel does not just sell music to churches. It offers music as a ministry resource.

### The Ministry Offer (Standard for All Releases)
1. Free 7-Day Devotional Guide — available to anyone
2. Small Group Discussion Guide — available to church leaders
3. Sermon Illustration Notes — available to pastors
4. CCLI Registration — music available for church service use (when registered)
5. Choir arrangements — available for major tracks (when produced)

### Outreach to Pastors and Ministry Leaders
- Personal message, not mass email
- Lead with the resource, not the music
- Example: "I wanted you to have this devotional guide for your congregation. There's music with it, but the guide stands on its own."
- Never ask a pastor to promote the music. Offer a resource. Let them decide what to do with it.
- Follow up once, 2 weeks after initial contact. No more.

### Responding to Ministry Inquiries
When a church, ministry, or pastor asks about using the music:
- Respond within 24 hours
- Be generous — do not gatekeep the music from churches based on licensing fees at this stage
- Document every inquiry in the Ministry Impact Log (Document 06)
- Send the full Ministry Resource Packet (devotional guide, discussion guide, any available resources)
