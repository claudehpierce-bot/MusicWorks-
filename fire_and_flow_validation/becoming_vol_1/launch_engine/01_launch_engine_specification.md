## What Is the Fire & Flow Launch Engine?

The Launch Engine is the complete operational system that converts approved creative work — masters, artwork, lyrics, metadata — into a fully executed, market-facing album release. It is the execution layer of MusicWorks, built specifically for Fire & Flow Gospel and designed to be reused for every future release.

Where MusicWorks Core defines the pipeline and the agents, the Launch Engine defines the **campaign**: how the music reaches people, how the momentum is built and sustained, and how the ministry impact is measured alongside the commercial impact.

The Launch Engine is not about making music. It begins when the music is finished and the creative work is approved.

---

## The Launch Engine's Three Phases

Every Fire & Flow release moves through three phases:

### Phase 1: Build (90 days to 8 days before launch)
This phase is about preparation, not promotion. The work done here determines how strong launch day will be.

- Distribution submission and platform setup
- Spotify editorial pitch
- Press kit and media kit preparation
- Email list warming
- Audience teaser content
- Playlist outreach preparation
- Devotional and ministry package completion

### Phase 2: Launch (7 days before through 7 days after)
This is the highest-intensity period of the campaign. Every platform, every channel, every relationship is activated.

- Daily content across all platforms
- Email launch sequence firing
- Playlist pitches sent and tracked
- Launch day execution
- Active community engagement
- Press and media follow-up

### Phase 3: Sustain (Days 8 through Day 90)
Most independent releases fade to near-zero activity within two weeks of launch. The Sustain phase deliberately extends the campaign with lighter-touch, higher-meaning content.

- Weekly content cadence
- Devotional deep dives
- Ministry outreach (churches, small groups, prayer communities)
- Track-specific features
- Listener testimonial sharing
- Post-launch analytics review
- Lessons Learned compilation

## What the Launch Engine Requires From the Artist

The Launch Engine is a service system. It can only operate if the artist provides approved, complete inputs. The engine fails if inputs are incomplete, late, or unapproved.

### Non-Negotiable Inputs Before the Engine Starts
| Input | Status Needed |
|-------|--------------|
| Mastered audio files (all tracks) | Approved and QC-passed |
| Album artwork | Approved, distribution-ready resolution |
| Lyrics (all tracks) | Approved by theology reviewer |
| Metadata (all fields) | Complete, ISRC codes assigned |
| Credits and split sheets | Signed by all contributors |
| Publishing information | PRO affiliation confirmed |
| Release date | Locked — no changes after distribution submission |
| Distribution platforms | Confirmed and submitted |
| Marketing platforms | Accounts active and optimized |
| Ministry goals | Defined by founder |

### Inputs That Can Come Later (But Improve Outcomes)
| Input | Impact If Missing |
|-------|-----------------|
| Music video | Launch relies on lyric video or visualizer |
| Devotional guide | Ministry package is lighter |
| Press quotes or endorsements | Press kit is less compelling |
| Pre-existing email list | Launch email has limited reach |
| Existing social following | Organic launch is slower |

## What the Launch Engine Does Not Do

- It does not make creative decisions for the artist
- It does not write or revise lyrics
- It does not replace the Theological Integrity Review (Agent 03)
- It does not replace the Audio QC gate (Agent 04)
- It does not guarantee streaming numbers or chart performance
- It does not connect to live platforms automatically (this is a V1 document-based system)

## Launch Engine Operating Principles

### Principle 1: Human Execution, System Intelligence
The Launch Engine is a system, not a robot. It tells you exactly what to do and when. A human executes every action. No content goes out on autopilot.

### Principle 2: Ministry First, Commerce Second
Every campaign decision starts with the question: "What does this do for the listener spiritually?" If a tactic would improve streams but reduce spiritual authenticity, it is rejected. If a tactic serves both, it is prioritized.

### Principle 3: Repeatable Over Perfect
The first launch will not be perfect. The second launch will be better. The Launch Engine improves because every campaign ends with a Lessons Learned document that feeds directly into the next campaign's planning. The system learns because you record what you observe.

### Principle 4: One Project at a Time Gets Full Attention
When a project is in Phase 2 (Launch), no other major project begins Phase 1. The founder's attention is finite. Splitting it across two launches simultaneously reduces the quality of both.

### Principle 5: Metrics Serve the Mission
Stream counts, email open rates, and playlist placements are metrics, not goals. The goal is "believers are moved and the Gospel is spread." The metrics tell you whether the execution is working well enough to serve the goal. They are reported honestly, including when they disappoint.

## The Five Campaign Modes

The Launch Engine can be operated in five modes depending on resources, timeline, and objectives. Modes are selected by the founder before Phase 1 begins and govern the intensity of the campaign.

| Mode | Purpose | Intensity |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Standard | Sustainable baseline launch | Medium |
| Growth | Audience expansion focus | Medium-High |
| Blitz | Maximum output during launch week | High |
| Chart Push | Streaming threshold optimization | High — Commercial focus |
| Ministry Push | Church and ministry community activation | Medium — Ministry focus |

Full mode specifications are in Document 3: Campaign Engine Specification.

## Relationship to MusicWorks Core

The Launch Engine is the operational layer built on top of MusicWorks Core. The Core pipeline (Agents 01–13) produces the deliverables. The Launch Engine executes them.

```
MusicWorks Core (Agents 01-13)
        ↓
  Approved Outputs
        ↓
Fire & Flow Launch Engine
        ↓
  Live Campaign Execution
        ↓
  Lessons Learned → feeds back into next project's Agent 01
```

The Launch Engine does not replace Core. It picks up where Core's final output (the Founder Approval Dashboard) ends.
