# Campaign KPI Dashboard Specification
**Project:** Becoming Vol. 1 — Fire & Flow Gospel
**Document:** Deliverable 6 of 9
**Version:** 1.0

---

> This document defines what to measure, where to find it, how often to pull it, and what the numbers mean for a new independent gospel artist. Numbers without context are noise. This dashboard turns numbers into decisions.

---

## DASHBOARD PHILOSOPHY

Two types of metrics matter in this campaign:

**Commercial Metrics** — Are people listening? Is the music being discovered? Is revenue accumulating?

**Ministry Metrics** — Is the music doing spiritual work? Are people engaging with the devotional content? Are churches and ministries adopting this music?

Both sets of metrics are reported. Neither overrides the other. A release with 50,000 streams and zero ministry impact is not a MusicWorks success. A release with 500 streams and 200 devotional downloads and 5 churches using it in services is. Know the difference.

## SECTION 1: STREAMING METRICS

**Source:** Spotify for Artists (primary), Apple Music for Artists, distributor dashboard

### Metrics to Track

| Metric | What It Means | Where to Find It | How Often |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Total Streams | Cumulative plays across all platforms | Distributor dashboard | Weekly |
| Spotify Daily Streams | Day-by-day listening activity | Spotify for Artists | Daily (launch week); Weekly (after) |
| Spotify Monthly Listeners | Unique listeners in rolling 28-day window | Spotify for Artists | Weekly |
| Spotify Saves | Listeners who saved the album or a track | Spotify for Artists | Weekly |
| Playlist Adds | How many playlists the music has been added to | Spotify for Artists | Weekly |
| Skip Rate | Percentage of listeners who skip before 30 seconds | Spotify for Artists | Weekly (watch for high skip rate — it's a problem) |
| Listener Geography | Where listeners are located | Spotify for Artists → Audience | Monthly |
| Apple Music Plays | Streams on Apple Music | Apple Music for Artists | Weekly |
| YouTube Music Streams | Via distributor dashboard | Distributor | Monthly |

### Benchmarks for a New Independent Gospel Artist

| Timeframe | Realistic Benchmark | Strong Performance |
|-----------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Day 1 | 50–200 streams | 500+ |
| Week 1 | 200–800 streams | 2,000+ |
| Month 1 | 1,000–3,000 streams | 5,000+ |
| Month 3 | 3,000–8,000 cumulative | 15,000+ |
| Monthly Listeners (Month 3) | 200–500 | 1,000+ |

**Important Context:** These benchmarks reflect organic performance with no paid promotion and a new audience. They are honest, not discouraging. Most signed artists have marketing teams, budgets, and existing audiences of millions. Compare to yourself from one release to the next — not to others.

### Red Flags
- Streams declining week-over-week after Week 2 with no explanation → Need a fresh content push
- Skip rate above 40% on a specific track → First 30 seconds of that track may need a listening review
- Monthly listeners declining while streams increase → The same small group is on repeat; new discovery has stalled
- No playlist adds by Week 3 → Playlist outreach needs a second wave

## SECTION 2: YOUTUBE METRICS

**Source:** YouTube Studio

| Metric | What It Means | How Often |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| Views (total) | Total video plays | Weekly |
| Watch Time (average) | How much of the video people watch | Weekly — aim for >50% |
| Click-Through Rate (CTR) | How many people clicked after seeing the thumbnail | Weekly — target 4–8% for music |
| Impressions | How many times YouTube showed the video | Weekly |
| Subscriber Growth | New subscribers from this video | Monthly |
| Shorts Views | Views on short-form content | Weekly |
| Top Performing Video | Which video has the highest views | Monthly |
| Geographic Distribution | Where viewers are located | Monthly |

### Benchmarks

| Timeframe | Realistic | Strong |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Week 1 | 100–500 views | 1,000+ |
| Month 1 | 500–2,000 total | 5,000+ |
| Average watch time | >40% | >60% |
| CTR | 2–5% | 5–10% |

### Red Flags
- Average watch time below 30% → Thumbnail or title is misleading; the audience expected something different
- CTR below 2% → Thumbnail is not compelling; A/B test a new one
- Zero Shorts views → The Shorts are not being indexed; check format requirements (vertical, under 60 seconds)

## SECTION 3: EMAIL METRICS

**Source:** Email platform (Mailchimp, ConvertKit, Flodesk, etc.)

| Metric | What It Means | How Often |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| Open Rate | % of recipients who opened the email | Per send |
| Click-Through Rate | % who clicked a link in the email | Per send |
| List Growth | New subscribers since last report | Weekly |
| Unsubscribe Rate | % who unsubscribed | Per send — high rate is a warning |
| Best Performing Email | Highest open + click rate | Monthly |
| Best Subject Line | Subject line with highest open rate | Monthly |

### Benchmarks

| Metric | Industry Average | Target for This Campaign |
|--------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Open Rate | 20–25% | 28–35% (gospel/ministry audiences tend higher) |
| Click Rate | 2–4% | 4–8% |
| List Growth (Month 1) | N/A | +30–50 subscribers |
| Unsubscribe Rate | <0.5% | <0.3% |

### Red Flags
- Open rate below 20% → Subject lines need review; list may need re-engagement
- Click rate below 2% → CTA is buried or unclear; email may have too many links competing
- Unsubscribe rate above 1% on any single email → That email's tone, content, or frequency was wrong
- List not growing → Landing page or sign-up incentive is not working

## SECTION 4: SOCIAL MEDIA METRICS

**Source:** Platform analytics (Instagram Insights, Facebook Analytics, TikTok Analytics)

| Metric | What It Means | How Often |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| Reach | How many unique accounts saw a post | Per post / Weekly |
| Engagement Rate | Likes + Comments + Shares ÷ Reach | Weekly |
| Saves (Instagram) | People who bookmarked the post — high-intent signal | Weekly |
| Shares / Reposts | How often content is shared by others | Weekly |
| Profile Visits from Post | People who visited the profile after seeing the post | Weekly |
| Follower Growth | Net new followers | Weekly |
| Best Performing Post | Highest engagement or reach | Monthly |
| Best Performing Format | Reel vs. static vs. carousel | Monthly |

### Benchmarks

| Metric | Acceptable | Strong |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Engagement Rate (Instagram) | 1–3% | 4%+ |
| Engagement Rate (Facebook) | 0.5–2% | 3%+ |
| Saves (as % of likes) | 5–10% | 15%+ |
| Follower Growth (Month 1) | +25–75 | +150+ |

### Red Flags
- Engagement rate below 1% consistently → Content is not resonating with current audience; try different formats or topics
- Low saves relative to likes → People "like" passively but don't find it valuable enough to save; increase utility and depth of content
- Follower count growing but engagement rate declining → Gaining followers who are not genuinely interested; review targeting

## SECTION 5: MINISTRY IMPACT METRICS

**Source:** Email inquiries, direct messages, CCLI dashboard, personal tracking

These metrics are tracked manually. They are not automated. The founder or a team member records every ministry impact event as it happens.

| Metric | Definition | How to Track |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| Devotional Guide Downloads | Total downloads from all distribution points | Platform dashboard (Gumroad, website, etc.) |
| Church/Ministry Inquiries | Any pastor, worship leader, or ministry organization that contacts us about using the music | Manual log |
| Confirmed Church Uses | Music used in a service, prayer meeting, or event (reported to us) | Manual log |
| Small Group Uses | Devotional guide used in a small group context (reported to us) | Manual log |
| Unsolicited Testimonials | Personal messages about spiritual impact received via DM, email, or comment | Manual log |
| CCLI Stream Count | Uses registered through CCLI if registered | CCLI dashboard |
| Sermon/Teaching Mentions | Music or lyrics referenced in a sermon or teaching | Manual log (self-reported or discovered) |

### Ministry Impact Log Template
*(Record each event as it occurs)*

### Ministry Impact Benchmarks

There are no industry benchmarks for these metrics because no other platform tracks them. MusicWorks is setting its own standards.

**Month 1 Targets (Becoming Vol. 1, V1):**
- Devotional downloads: 25+
- Church/Ministry inquiries: 2+
- Unsolicited testimonials: 5+
- Confirmed church uses: 1+

## SECTION 6: REVENUE METRICS

**Source:** Distributor royalty dashboard, PRO statement, SoundExchange, direct sales platform

| Metric | Source | Frequency |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Streaming royalties (total) | Distributor | Monthly (usually 30–60 days delayed) |
| Streaming royalties by platform | Distributor | Monthly |
| YouTube ContentID revenue | Distributor or YouTube | Monthly |
| PRO performance royalties | ASCAP/BMI | Quarterly |
| SoundExchange royalties | SoundExchange | Semi-annual |
| Direct sales (Bandcamp/Gumroad) | Platform dashboards | Monthly |
| Total revenue (all sources) | Manual compilation | Monthly |

### Revenue Benchmarks (Realistic, Month 1–3)

| Source | Month 1–3 Estimate |
|--------|-------------------|
| Spotify/Apple royalties | $3–$30 (1,000–10,000 streams at ~$0.003/stream) |
| YouTube | Minimal unless ContentID is active |
| Direct sales | $0–$100 (if Bandcamp is set up and promoted) |
| Devotional guide | $0–$50 (if sold; $0 if free) |
| PRO royalties | $0 in Month 1–3 (typically delays 6–12 months for first payment) |
| **Total realistic M1–3** | **$3–$180** |

**Note to Founder:** Month 1–3 streaming revenue is almost always below $50 for a new independent artist. This is not failure. It is the reality of streaming economics. Revenue builds over time as catalog grows, algorithmic placements accumulate, and audience compounds. The financial return of a catalog is 2–5 years, not 90 days.
