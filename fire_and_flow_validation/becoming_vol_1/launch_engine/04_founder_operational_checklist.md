## The Founder's Two Roles

In this campaign, you wear two hats that must never be confused with each other:

**Hat 1: The Artist / Minister**
You are the creative and spiritual voice of Fire & Flow Gospel. Decisions about what the music means, what the message is, and whether the campaign represents the Gospel faithfully — these belong to you alone. They cannot be delegated.

**Hat 2: The Executive**
You are the CEO of MindSpark MusicWorks™ and the executive producer of this release. Strategic approvals, resource allocation, go/no-go decisions — these belong to you as the executive.

A third hat — **the Content Creator / Social Media Manager / Email Sender** — should be delegated as soon as resources allow. For V1, you may be wearing all three. That is acceptable for one release. It is not sustainable at scale.

## WEEKLY FOUNDER REVIEW SCHEDULE

Once the campaign is running, the founder's ongoing role is **governance, not execution**. The following reviews are non-negotiable.

### Monday Morning Review (15 minutes — every week for 12 weeks)
Pull these three numbers from Spotify for Artists and record them:
1. Weekly streams (this week vs. last week)
2. Monthly listeners (current count)
3. Number of saves this week

Answer one question: Is the trend up, flat, or down? If down for two consecutive weeks, schedule a Campaign Review meeting with yourself (or your team).

### Monthly Campaign Review (60 minutes — first Monday of each month)
Pull the full KPI dashboard (Document 6). Answer:
1. Are we meeting the 30-day benchmarks for our current mode?
2. What is the highest-performing content piece this month? Why did it work?
3. What is the most meaningful ministry moment this month? (A testimonial, an inquiry, a church use)
4. What one change would I make to the campaign if I started again today?
5. Should we change campaign modes?

Record your answers in the Lessons Learned running document.

### End-of-Campaign Review (3 hours — Day 90)
Full Lessons Learned document. See Document 7.

## THE FOUNDER'S LAUNCH DAY PERSONAL ROLE

On July 3, 2026, the founder's most important job is not metrics management. It is:

1. **Show up spiritually.** Begin the day with prayer or devotional time before the album goes live. This is a ministry moment, not a product launch.

2. **Be present for 6–8 hours.** Respond personally to every message, comment, and mention you receive on launch day. The first day sets the relationship tone with your audience. 

3. **Speak.** Go live, record a voice memo, post a personal video, or write a personal note. Let the audience hear from the artist, not just the marketing. One moment of genuine personal presence on launch day is worth more than 10 scheduled posts.

4. **Receive the day.** Something unexpected will happen. A stranger will message you something that breaks your heart open. A pastor will share the album without being asked. A lyric will land for someone in a way you never anticipated. Stay awake to these moments. They are the real data.

5. **Write it down.** Before you sleep on July 3, write one paragraph about what this day meant. File it in the Lessons Learned document. You will be grateful for this paragraph when you are preparing the next album.

## FOUNDER ENERGY MANAGEMENT

A launch week at Blitz intensity is physically and emotionally demanding. Plan accordingly.

**Before launch week:**
- Clear your calendar of non-essential meetings the week of July 3
- Communicate to collaborators that this week requires focused attention
- Prepare meals, rest, and personal time — a depleted founder makes poor decisions

**During launch week:**
- Set specific times for checking metrics (morning and evening — not constantly)
- Set specific times for responding to messages (morning, midday, evening — not constantly)
- Take one 30-minute break each day that is completely offline

**After launch week:**
- Celebrate. This is the first MusicWorks validation launch. Regardless of the numbers, the system ran. That is worth acknowledging.
